The effects of water temperature and exposure
area by water on human responses and bathing time

This thesis is about effect on human response while bathing
in several conditions. The experiments are conducted with 4 conditions,
38ºC-abdomen, 38ºC-chest, 40ºC-chest, and 42ºC-chest to measure
skin temperature, rectal temperature, heart rate, sweat rate, and blood pressure.
The skin temperature and rectal temperature results were higher
as water temperature was higher. The heart rate results were high
while bathing 40ºC and 42ºC. They were barely affected by water amount.
The blood pressure while rest after bathing is lower than while bathing.
The sweat rate results were higher while bathing 40ºC and 42ºC, and
as temperature was high, the results were high while bathing. Subjects who
are larger BMI took longer bath time. In addition, Subjects whose heart
rate is usually high took shorter bath time.
